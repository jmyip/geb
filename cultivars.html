Varieties

BLUECROP

The bestselling variety within the industry. Its size, productivity, flavors, and quality makes it a favorite in the fresh market. Growers also prefer this berry for its easy accessibility to mechanical harvesting, and low handpicking expenses in relation to other popular varieties. Bluecrop consistently bears immense quantities of medium-large fruit that are drought resistant, and widely adapted.

BLUEGOLD

Blue Gold’s bushes are high producers of superior berries. Tests conducted on the cultivar found that its shelf life in normal storage as well as CA Storage is outstanding. Bluegold is one of the easiest varieties for hand harvest and a good candidate for mechanical harvesting.

BRIGITTA BLUE

Originally from Australia, the Brigitta Blue is grown for the fresh market. It is a late developer; however it is a great producing blueberry. It is mechanically harvested, ships and stores well.

DUKE

The leading northern highbush variety in the West. It is known for its high yields and excellent quality fruit. Mechanical harvesting has been very successful the last few years with growers producing fresh market quality fruit. Best variety for fresh market and dessert quality frozen product.

Harvest Period Type of Market Fruit Flavor

ELLIOTT

Although not large in size, Elliotts are very aesthetically and productively attractive. It is the last variety that is machine-harvested in one or two pickings. They are extremely resilient and produce consistent large yields of berries. Elliot is presently the best highbush variety for CA storage, which is extending the sellable shelf life for up to 12 weeks.

Harvest Period Type of Market Fruit Flavor

HARDY BLUE (1613-A)

Hardy Blue can be mechanically harvested in one or two pickings. The fruit does not drop quickly once ripe, which makes it easier for berry growers to machine pick their crop. Given its tendency to shrivel when fully ripe, the Hardy Blue is not a suitable cultivar for long-distance fresh shipping. Its fair quality is however excellent for processing.

JERSEY

Jersey is one of the oldest varieties and one of the most dependable producers. It keeps well and its quality for dessert-type foods is fair. Its berries can grow up to 22.4mm in diameter, and until ripe is too acid for the taste of most persons. It is mechanically harvested for the processed market that find small to medium-sized berries desirable.

JUNE

June is of the same parentage as Rancocas. In commercial plantations, the berries have sometimes exceeded 21 mm in diameter. Although one-fourth of June’s genetic makeup is lowbush, its leaf margins do not have the aspect that characterize its lowbush parentage.
